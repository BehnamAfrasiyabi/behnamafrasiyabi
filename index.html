Hello, I'm Behnam Afrasiyabi,
Electronic Design Engineer

Hardware designer with extensive experience across automotive, industrial, and medical sectors, specializing in embedded systems for mission-critical applications. During my academic studies, I developed a smartphone-based smart home system for remote appliance control. I later designed a test and simulation platform for in-vehicle sensors and contributed to the development of an automotive diagnostic tool and ECU troubleshooter. Following graduation, I began my professional career in the manufacturing, assembly, and soldering of industrial printed circuit boards. I am currently employed at MAPNA Electric & Control (MECO) as part of the MAPNA Automation and Process Control System (MAPCS)—the company’s state-of-the-art DCS/FCS platform. In my current role, I serve as **Team Lead for Product Development** and **Hardware Concept Designer**, where I spearhead the ideation, architecture, and development of next-generation hardware solutions for industrial control systems, including Distributed Control Systems (DCS), Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC), Remote Terminal Units (RTU), and related control infrastructure. My responsibilities encompass technical leadership, system-level design, and cross-functional coordination to deliver robust, scalable, and reliable hardware platforms aligned with industry standards and operational requirements.
